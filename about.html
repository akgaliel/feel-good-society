The Gap We Saw

Why We Started

Feel Good Society was born from a simple observation: so many people living with disabilities or mental health challenges were spending their weekends at home — not because they wanted to, but because the social world wasn't built for them. Too loud, too unpredictable, too much pressure to explain yourself before you'd even sat down.

We believed that was worth changing. So we did. Monthly dinner events, designed from the ground up to feel safe, fun, and genuinely welcoming to everyone who walks through the door.

Why Dinners

More Than a Meal

Dinners work because food puts everyone on equal footing. There's no pressure to stand around awkwardly — you sit, you eat, you talk. We add live entertainment and activities because great conversation shouldn't feel forced. Games break the ice. Music lifts the room.

By the time dessert arrives, people who came in as strangers are already planning to come back together next month. That's the feeling we're chasing, every single event.
